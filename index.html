Scout: Who's Raising?

Like other founders, I get approached often by entrepreneurs looking for help raising their next round. If they’re good, I introduce them to my investors. Most closed venture deals start out like this – they’re network referrals from people investors trust.

As an investor, you miss out on a lot of deals simply because you don’t find out about them. They’re never even referred to you because they're outside your network.

Scout is a new way for investors to avoid missing deals. We combine introductions from a network of founders and send them to our backers while they're still fresh. This helps everyone: vetted entrepreneurs get in front of more investors and nobody misses out on the best deals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who's in the scout network?

A few YC founders.

How do you source deals?

Founders making introductions to their own investors can choose to do that through Scout more quickly than writing several emails.

Why are you doing this?

Financial returns in startup investing are weighted heavily towards outliers. Missing outliers is costly, so information about who's fundraising is valuable.

Why are you really doing this?

Fundraising is a pain and the early-stage investing market is illiquid. By combining information from multiple dealflow sources, you can create a more liquid market in the early stages.
